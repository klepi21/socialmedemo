# Business Strategy & Pricing Advice

This document provides guidance on how to package and price this project for your client.

## 1. Value Proposition
Instead of "hiring someone full-time for €1,500/month," you are offering a **Capital Investment** that:
- **Reduces Overhead**: This bot replaces the need for a junior sales/admin role focused on intake.
- **Works 24/7**: Unlike an employee, the bot qualifies leads while the team sleeps.
- **Higher Consistency**: The bot never forgets to ask about the budget or location.
- **Scalability**: It can handle 100 clients at once; an employee cannot.

## 2. Competitive Pricing Model
Given the client's current budget expectation (€1,500/mo salary), you should pitch this as a **Setup Fee + Monthly Maintenance**.

### Option A: The "Project Buyout" (Higher Upfront)
- **Setup Fee**: €2,500 - €3,500 (Designing the UI, Training the Brain, Dashboard setup).
- **Maintenance**: €200 - €300/month (Covers API costs, small updates, hosting, and knowledge base refreshes).
- *Client Perspective*: They pay 2 months of salary upfront but save €1,200/month forever.

### Option B: The "Productized Service" (Lower Upfront)
- **Setup Fee**: €900.
- **Monthly Subscription**: €500 - €700/month.
- *Client Perspective*: Lower risk, but you get a recurring "salary-like" income without the full-time commitment.

## 3. How to Demo (The "Hook")
1. **Mockup**: Create a simple Next.js page with a beautiful chatbox (using the free tiers mentioned).
2. **Knowledge Proof**: Scrape *their* actual website and let them chat with it. When the bot says "We offer React development and our office is in [City]," they will be "wow-ed."
3. **The PDF Summary**: Show them a "fake" PDF summary generated from a 2-minute conversation. This is the "killer feature" for a business owner.

## 4. Key Questions to Ask the Client
- "What is your typical 'intake' form looking like now?"
- "Which services are the hardest to explain to clients?"
- "What is your average lead value?" (If one lead = €5,000, then the bot pays for itself with just ONE sale).
